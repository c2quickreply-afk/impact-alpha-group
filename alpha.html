The thesis

Capital is powerful. Alignment is everything. The greatest constraint in complex initiatives is rarely money — it is the alignment between leadership, capital sources, strategic partners, and the many stakeholders required to move an initiative forward. When those elements fall into sync, momentum compounds. That is where we come in.

The people

Trust, cultivated over 25 years.

Francene operates at the intersection of capital, relationships, and execution. She began her career in institutional equity trading and sales — more than twenty-five years in financial markets where sound judgment, disciplined execution, and trusted relationships were essential — before turning that rigor toward the advancement of complex, mission-driven initiatives.

She went on to found Schoolyard Crop, a nonprofit that brought environmental education into schools through edible gardens and systems thinking — work that joined fundraising, community engagement, and partnership development around a shared purpose. That same combination now lets her align capital partnerships behind regenerative development, community resilience, and sustainable economic systems.

Her consistent strength: cultivating trust quickly and building durable relationships across sectors. Many of the partnerships she coordinates begin with a single introduction — and evolve into long-term strategic alignment.
